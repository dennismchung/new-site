Kiddom for Families

At Kiddom, our mission is to enable teachers and learners. We sought to extend our services by providing parents a view into their child’s progress in their classes.

Overview

As such we want to help students and teachers by providing family members (guardians, parents, etc.) ongoing views into student progress. Kiddom for Families V1 is our first MVP to accomplish this goal. There is extensive research to support the premise that student outcomes are improved through support at home. As such enabling access to student performance for families supports this.

Research:

Multiple Schools who were using Kiddom and also potential clients expressed an interest in a parent and student portal. Kiddom chose to do a study at Marshall County school where we talked to parents, teachers, and students.

We wanted to research this implementation and explore broader themes of communication between school and home from teachers, students and parents.

Research Session takeaways:

  • Quickly understand how their child is doing.
  • Providing information in a timely matter to the parent.
  • Parents wanted to see what assignments their children are working on. How they are progressing and where they’ll need extra help.

Flow of inside of a family view

From the research session at Marshall County, we decided on a rough MVP that would provide a view for multiple students, multiple classes and can toggle between a Reports view and Timeline view.

Quick and Easy

We wanted to focus on class assignments because our core product revolves around teachers assigning content to students. Unpacking per-standard mastery is likely over parents' heads, so we needed to find a way to make it easy to digest and understand how their child was doing.

We came up with a home screen that will have show a list of the parents children, classes they are in, and a short summary of their progress. We knew that parents would want to quickly see how their child was doing from the research sessions.

Cross assignment filtering

We also planned to display assignments by classes and by Upcoming, Overdue and Recent. Parents would want to filter their childs assignments by urgency (what’s due? Where does my child need help the most? ..etc)

View Only

We also recieved feedback from teachers about showing too much information to the parents (EXPLAIN!). By only allowing the parent to see the assignment and the childs progress.

Challenges

Time Constraints, We wanted to get this out before schools went on winter break, so we moved very quickly. Since school admins needed an onboarding process for parents, we would have required even more time before the release. Had to sacrifice user testing and Research time was cut.

Loading Problems

We actually encountered a technical constraint pitfall during development. Since this was a stand-alone feature of the product we were in uncharted territory. Initially, we wanted to have a combined view of all the classes and assignments a student had.

However, the way our backend data was built made it very difficult to see assignments from multiple classes in one view. It would take around 30 seconds - 1 minute for data to load. Given the tight timeline, we decided to pivot to a single class view.

Compromise

No longer designing an aggregate view of class assignments based off status, I wanted to still focus on creating an experience for the parent to easily understand their childs progress at a glance. I added the # of overdue and upcoming attributes in the class card on the home screen.

Segment Navigation

  1. I wanted to show the parent an intuitive view of the childs progress. 3 main components: The childs grade over a range of time, Progression (delta of current grade and grade 2 weeks ago) from the last 2 weeks, and graph to help visualize progress.
2

Since we could not support an aggregate view of assignment status', I designed a segment view so that the parent can easily navigate each category.

3

Kiddom supports multiple grading types that might confuse parents. By providing an informational link that will educate what their childs grade means.

4

Another signal to help the parent see that their child needs help in a class is the notification badge for overdue assignments.

    Conclusion

    This project was one of my favorites to work on at Kiddom. Since this was a stand-alone feature (separate from the teacher and student experience) I was able to create something new and push our apps design boundaries. Things like:

    • How a parent would want to best navigate and view their children’s assignments
    • Creating a new intuitive way for parents to understand their childs grade.

    Some things planned for our future releases:

    • Fix our backend architecture so that student data would load quicker.
    • Clearer visual language to understand a complex idea of Mastery.
    • Teacher Parent communication
    • Detailed behavioral/social progress. Note-taking and reporting etc.
